Red-green colour blindness affects about 8% of men and 0.5% of women, making it the most common colour vision deficiency. People with it struggle to distinguish reds, greens, and related shades. Everyday tasks—like reading colour-coded charts or traffic lights—can be challenging, but accessible design can greatly improve usability.
